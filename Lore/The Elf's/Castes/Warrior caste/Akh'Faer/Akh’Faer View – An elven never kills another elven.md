\## \*\*Akh’Faer View – “An elven never kills another elven”\*\*



\*\*Philosophical Stance:\*\*

The Akh’Faer uphold this tradition without hesitation in peace.

They teach that unity among the \*People of Magic\* is the keystone of civilisation — the Flow cannot endure if elves turn on one another. Every student of the Akh’Faer learns from their first lesson that this law is sacred because it maintains resonance, trust, and the shared rhythm of magical harmony.



\*\*Interpretation in War:\*\*

In war, the Akh’Faer interpret this law as \*conditional upon loyalty\*.

An elf who betrays the People — who leaks secrets, sabotages defences, or aids the enemy — has, by that act, already severed their link to the Flow.

Such an individual is no longer counted among the elven.

To the Akh’Faer, treason constitutes \*\*instant excommunication\*\*, even before formal rites can be held.



\*\*Practical Doctrine:\*\*

Once the betrayal is proven or witnessed beyond doubt, the traitor is deemed \*already fallen beyond the current\*.

The Akh’Faer will act swiftly and without sentiment.

They do not debate or hesitate — they remove the threat as efficiently as possible.

Killing such a one is not considered breaking the law, because \*\*no elf has been slain\*\*; the Flow has already rejected the traitor.

Their death is an act of preservation, not vengeance.



\*\*Rationale:\*\*



> “The traitor dies before the body falls.

> We merely finish what their soul began.”

> — Akh’Faer Commander Eryndiel Thalanir, War of the Crimson Ash



\*\*Example:\*\*

During the Siege of Miriel’s Veil, an elven signal officer attempted to send the fortress’s wards to the enemy.

Before his message could be completed, an Akh’Faer spell-lancer struck him down in full view of the command post.

No inquiry followed; the council simply recorded: \*“Resonance Severed by Choice.”\*

The act was never counted as elven bloodshed, for the current had already withdrawn from the traitor’s body.



\*\*Summary of the Akh’Faer Doctrine:\*\*



\* The law \*“An elven never kills another elven”\* remains absolute for the loyal.

\* The moment an elf betrays the People, the Flow severs them — excommunication by consequence, not ceremony.

\* Once cut from the current, they are no longer of the elven kind.

\* Eliminating such a being is not killing, but \*restoring equilibrium\*.

\* Prolonging their life only risks corruption of the Flow.



\*\*Codex Annotation (used in the War Archives):\*\*



> \*“Tradition guards the heart; necessity guards the world.

> When one betrays both, mercy is waste.”\*
