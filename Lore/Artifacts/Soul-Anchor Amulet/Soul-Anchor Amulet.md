# Major Artifact: The Soul-Anchor Amulet (The Leaking Vessel)

**Description:**  
The Soul-Anchor Amulet is a polished obsidian locket shaped like a hourglass, but instead of sand, a faint, swirling grey mist (the stolen souls) moves within the glass. It pulses with a warm, living heat when charged, and feels like a block of ice when empty.

## The Law of Equilibrium

### The Power: Death Denied
The Soul-Anchor Amulet allows the wielder to bypass death by substituting their own end with the stolen "time" of others.

1.  **The Seven-Life Reservoir:** The amulet can hold up to 7 "Soul Charges." These slots are empty when the amulet is first put on or if it is ever removed and re-equipped.
2.  **Cold-Blooded Charging:** To "charge" a slot, the wielder must murder a humanoid in cold blood (killing a helpless or innocent creature) while wearing the amulet.
3.  **Automatic Resurgence:** If the wielder is reduced to 0 HP or struck by a death effect, the amulet immediately consumes the **oldest** soul-charge. The wielder is restored to full health and all negative conditions are removed.
4.  **Biological Freeze:** As long as at least one soul-charge remains, the wielder does not age.

### The Burden: The Price of the Hunt (The ONLY Price)
The amulet does not consume the wielder's soul—it simply requires a constant supply of others'.

1.  **Soul Decay (3d100 Days):** Every soul trapped within the amulet is on borrowed time. When a soul is captured, the DM (or player) rolls **3d100**. This is the number of days that specific soul will remain in the amulet.
2.  **The Expiration:** Once a soul's individual duration expires, it vanishes from the amulet. It can no longer be used for an "extra life" and no longer prevents the wielder's aging.
3.  **The Eternal Hunter:** Because of the random duration (3d100 days), the wielder can never be sure when their protection will fail. They are forced to keep killing to ensure they always have a "fresh" soul in the reservoir.

## Lore and Tales

### The Tale of the Eternal Merchant
There was once a merchant who feared the end so much he bought this amulet from a dark stranger. 
He lived for three hundred years, never aging a day, but he was forced to travel from town to town, always leaving one "disappearance" behind him. One night, 
while crossing a deserted wasteland where no humans lived, all seven of his souls expired at once. By the time he reached the next city, he was nothing but a pile of fine white dust inside a suit of expensive silk clothes.

### The Identity of the Dark Stranger
While the "Dark Stranger" in the merchant's tale is often described as a lone wanderer, scholars of the arcane know that the Soul-Anchor Amulet has passed through dozens of hands over the centuries—kings, cowards, and criminals alike. The true origin of the "Dark Stranger" lies with **The Gilded Harvesters**, a secret society of Pixies.

**Sylas of the Bitter-Grin** is the member most often linked to the amulet's distribution. He (and others of his cabal) does not sell the amulet to help the buyer; he plants it like a seed. They appear to those whose fear of death has curdled into an obsession, offering them "The Leaking Vessel" (the Amulet's true name).

### The Price of Extreme Value
Pixies of the Gilded Harvesters do not trade in coin; they trade in the "Weight of the Self." To purchase the Soul-Anchor Amulet, one must pay a price of **Extreme Value**:
1.  **The True Name:** The buyer forgets their own name the moment the deal is struck. To the world, they become a ghost with a face.
2.  **The Capacity for Regret:** To ensure the wielder can perform the "Cold-Blooded Charging" required, the Pixie strips away their ability to feel guilt.

### The Pixie's Motivation: The Harvest
The "Harvest" is a closely guarded secret among the Pixie courts. The **Soul Decay** (the 3d100 day limit) is not a flaw, but the purpose of the artifact.
*   **The Siphon:** When a soul vanishes from the Amulet, it is funneled back to the Feywild. 
*   **The Consumption:** The Gilded Harvesters "drink" the memories and experiences of the souls captured by the wielder. This grants them forbidden knowledge but often drives them into a state of "Bitter-Grin" madness as they struggle to contain thousands of discordant mortal lives.
*   **Extreme Danger:** This group is considered **EXTREMELY dangerous** even by other Pixies. They are known to aggressively use their memory-wiping abilities to "hollow out" anyone who discovers the truth of the Harvest.
