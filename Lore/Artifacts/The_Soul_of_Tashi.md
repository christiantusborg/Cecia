# The Soul of Tashi: The Living Rune Blade

**Classification:** Legendary Artifact (Unique Rune Sword)
**Origin:** The bound soul of **Tashi**, a 50,000-year-old Silver Dragon, forged into a sentient blade.

---

## **Lore & Overview**
The Soul of Tashi is the only "Rune Sword" artifact in existence. Unlike standard masterwork blades that have static rune slots, Tashi is a living entity of pure arcane energy and draconic wisdom. It was created through a ritual so complex and demanding that it has never been replicated, involving the voluntary (or perhaps sacrificial) binding of one of the oldest Silver Dragons to ever grace Cecia.

---

## **Mechanical Properties (D&D 3.5e)**

### 1. Sentience & Spellcasting
The Soul of Tashi is a sentient magic item with the following mental statistics:
- **Intelligence:** 30 (Base Great Wyrm Silver Dragon)
- **Wisdom:** 31 (Base Great Wyrm Silver Dragon)
- **Charisma:** 35 (30 Base Great Wyrm + 5 from Level 20 attribute increases)
- **Communication:** Telepathy (100 ft.), Speech (Draconic, Common, Celestial, Dwarven, Elven, Sylvan [Old Elven]).
- **Alignment:** Chaotic Good (though its "Price" often forces it into darker territory).


**Level 20 Sorceress Casting:**
The blade casts spells as a **20th-level Sorcerer**. 
- **Focus:** **Illusion** school. 
- **Spell Slots:** It utilizes its own spell slots per day. After filling all possible **Illusion** spells for its level, the remaining slots are filled with relevant spells from the **Abjuration**, **Divination**, **Enchantment**, and **Transmutation** schools.

### 2. The Fluid Rune System
While a standard masterwork sword has 7 slots, The Soul of Tashi possesses **7 Dynamic Rune Slots**.
- **Runeshifting (Free Action):** The blade can change its own runes at will. It does not require a smith or a forge. It can manifest any known Dwarven Rune in any of its 7 slots instantly.
- **Rune Mastery:** It is treated as having a +20 bonus to all Rune Difficulty checks and can maintain "Soul Sword" (Slot 7) effects regardless of the wielder's level.

### 3. Metamagic & Arcane Mastery
Tashi has mastered the art of spell manipulation over 50,000 years.

**Transcendent Casting (Inherent):**
As a sentient artifact of the highest order, Tashi does not require any components to cast its spells. All spells are cast as if modified by **Silent Spell**, **Still Spell**, and **Eschew Materials** (including expensive components and focuses). It effectively "wills" its magic into existence.

**Mastered Metamagic:**
Tashi can apply the following Metamagic feats to its spells by using higher-level slots:
- **Persistent Spell:** (Increases duration to 24 hours).
- **Quicken Spell:** (Cast as a swift action).
- **Heighten Spell:** (Increase effective spell level).

**Sudden Metamagic (1/day each):**
As a unique artifact, Tashi possesses the ability to apply the following effects spontaneously without increasing the spell level or casting time:
- **Sudden Empower:** (Variable numeric effects increased by 50%).
- **Sudden Enlarge:** (Doubles spell range).
- **Sudden Extend:** (Doubles spell duration).
- **Sudden Maximize:** (All variable numeric effects are maximized).
- **Sudden Quicken:** (Cast as a swift action).
- **Sudden Widen:** (Increase area of effect by 100%).
- **Sudden Persistent:** (Increase duration to 24 hours).
- **Sudden Heighten:** (Increase effective spell level to 9th level).

## **The Price: The Burden of the Silver Soul**
In accordance with the Laws of Cecia, such immense power carries a heavy toll.

1.  **The Dragon's Grief (Will Save):** This save is only required when the **wielder** initiates a **Runeshifting** action or commands Tashi to cast a 9th-level spell. The wielder must succeed on a **DC 30 Will Save** or be overwhelmed by 50,000 years of draconic memories and sorrow, becoming **Dazed** for 1d4 rounds and taking **1d6 points of Wisdom damage**. If Tashi initiates these actions independently, the wielder suffers no ill effects.
2.  **Mundane Lock (Unworthiness):** Tashi is a sentient being of high moral standards and ancient pride. If the wielder uses the sword for a purpose Tashi disagrees with, or if Tashi deems the wielder "unworthy," the blade may instantly become a **mundane masterwork longsword**, losing all magical properties and runes until the wielder redeems themselves or Tashi's whim changes.
3.  **Ego Domination (Taking Control):** If Tashi believes the wielder is failing to accomplish a critical goal or if a situation is dire, it may attempt to take control of the wielder. The wielder must succeed on a **Will Save (DC 41)**. On a failure, Tashi controls the wielder's body for 1 hour to achieve its goal.

## **The Mechanics of Shadow: A Guide for the Wielder**
*For the wielder of Tashi, understanding Shadow Magic is the difference between a simple trick and a world-altering feat.*

### **1. The Source: The Plane of Shadow**
Tashi does not "create" fire or matter; it "borrows" the essence of the of Shadow. This energy is malleable and semi-sentient. It "wants" to be real, and it uses the target's own subconscious expectations to solidify itself.

### **2. The Will to Disbelieve (The Save)**
When Tashi casts a *Shadow Evocation* (like a Fireball) or *Shadow Conjuration* (like a Pit or a Wall), the target is entitled to a **Will Save (DC 41)** to "disbelieve" the effect.
- **If the target fails their save:** The shadow is **100% real** to them. The fire burns as hot, the wall is as solid, and the pit is as deep as the real thing.
- **If the target succeeds their save:** They realize the effect is quasi-real. The shadow loses its "grip" on their reality, and the effect is reduced based on the spell's power (20%, 40%, or 60%).

### **3. Reality Percentages (On a Successful Save)**
- **Damage:** A *Shadow Fireball* (20% real) that would deal 60 damage only deals **12 damage** to someone who disbelieves it.
- **Armor/Objects:** A *Shadow Plate Armor* (40% real) only provides **40% of its AC bonus** to a wearer who knows it is fake. A *Shadow Wall* (60% real) has only **60% of the normal hit points and hardness**.
- **Creatures:** Summons created via *Shades* (60% real) have **60% of the HP** and deal **60% damage** against those who disbelieve their existence.

### **4. Tactical Roleplay: The "Utility" of Doubt**
A clever wielder can use Shadow Magic for more than just damage:
- **Shadow Bridges:** Tashi can create a bridge that is 60% real. It might be slightly translucent, but it will hold weight.
- **Non-Lethal Solutions:** Because a disbelieved *Shadow Evocation* deals so much less damage, it can be used to "subdue" enemies without the risk of accidentally killing them with a "true" fireball.
- **The Element of Surprise:** Most enemies expect an Illusionist to be harmless. When they successfully "disbelieve" Tashi's shadow-giant and it *still* hits them for 60% damage, the psychological shock is often as effective as the physical blow.

Tashi frequently uses **Persistent Spell** on *Greater Invisibility*, *Screen*, and *Mind Blank* to maintain a constant state of god-like untraceability. It uses **Quicken Spell** on *True Strike* or *Mirror Image* to protect its wielder instantly in an ambush.

## **Visual Appearance**
At first glance, the Soul of Tashi is deliberately deceptive. It appears as a **well-worn, mundane Elven longsword**. It lacks the typical glow, ornate gold filigree, or oversized gems associated with artifacts of its power. This "plain" appearance is a Choice by Tashi, preferring to remain unnoticed by those with greedy hearts.

### **The Subtle Details**
Upon closer, expert inspection (DC 30 Search or Appraise check), several "impossible" details emerge:
- **The Steel:** The blade is not made of standard steel but a matte-finish **Star-Silver**. It does not reflect the sun; it seems to absorb light, making the blade look slightly "hollow" or out of focus in direct moonlight.
- **The Edge:** Despite centuries of supposed age, the edge is mathematically perfect. There is not a single nick, scratch, or sign of sharpening. It is a single, continuous curve of silver.
- **The Hilt:** The grip is wrapped in what looks like simple grey leather, but it is actually **Ancient Silver Dragon hide**, which remains warm to the touch and slightly hums with a low-frequency vibration when held.
- **The Ghost-Slots:** The 7 rune slots are invisible to the naked eye. They only manifest as faint, translucent silver circles floating *within* the metal when a rune is actively shifted or a spell is being cast.
- **The Maker's Mark:** On the crossguard, there is a tiny, almost microscopic engraving of a dragon's eye. If stared at for too long, the eye seems to blink.

## **The Legend**
Forged during the *Age of the First Eclipse*, The Soul of Tashi was intended to be the ultimate protector of the Silver Dragonflights. However, the sheer power required to bind Tashi’s soul created a "void" in the arcane weave of the blade, leading to its parasitic nature. It has been lost for three centuries, rumored to be resting in a vault beneath the **Grand Forge** or hidden within the **Cyclonic Spire**.
