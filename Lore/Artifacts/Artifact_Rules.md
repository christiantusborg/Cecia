# Artifacts of Cecia: Power and the Price

In Cecia, artifacts are not merely magical items; they are fragments of divine will, remnants of ancient catastrophes, or the physical manifestations of cosmic concepts. While they offer immense power, they are inherently dangerous and often demand a steep price from those who would wield them.

## Classification of Artifacts

Artifacts in Cecia are classified into four primary tiers based on their potency and the magnitude of their effects on the world:

1.  **Minor Artifacts:** Powerful relics that can influence a single battle or region. Their effects are significant but localized.
2.  **Major Artifacts:** Instruments of immense power capable of altering the course of history for a nation or a continent.
3.  **Great Artifacts:** Legendary items that can reshape reality itself, often tied to deities or primordial forces.
4.  **Legendary Artifacts:** The rarest and most powerful objects in existence. Often unique and capable of defining entire eras.

## The Law of Equilibrium: Power with a Price

The fundamental rule of artifacts in Cecia is that **every boon must be balanced by a burden.** The more power an artifact grants, the more severe the consequence of its use.

### The Downside (The Price)
Except for the most exceedingly rare cases, every artifact possesses a detrimental effect or a significant drawback. This "Price" can manifest in several ways:

*   **Ability Drain/Damage:** Using the artifact's powers may temporarily or permanently drain the wielder's physical or mental attributes.
*   **Corruption/Curse:** Prolonged contact or use can lead to the corruption of the soul, physical mutations, or a specific curse that haunts the wielder.
*   **Compulsion/Obsession:** Artifacts often possess a limited form of sentience or a powerful aura that compels the wielder to act in certain ways, often leading to their own downfall.
*   **Environmental Toll:** The activation of an artifact might cause collateral damage to the surrounding land, drain life from nearby creatures, or tear the veil of reality.
*   **Specific Vunerability:** The wielder might gain a specific, debilitating weakness while attuned to or using the artifact.

### The "Anomalies": Positive-Only Artifacts
There are a **REALLY REALLY FEW** artifacts that provide only positive effects without an inherent downside. These are considered **EXTREMELY RARE** and are often the subject of myths and legends. Their existence is frequently debated by scholars, and finding one is considered a world-altering event.

## The Architects of Fate: The Pixie Connection

In Cecian folklore, while Dwarves are the masters of the forge and Elves are the stewards of ancient wisdom, it is the **Pixies** who are recognized as the primary creators of Major and Great Artifacts. Unlike mortal smiths, Pixies do not "make" items so much as they weave concepts, memories, and cosmic laws into physical forms.

### The Gilded Harvesters
The most prolific creators of these artifacts belong to a secret society known as **The Gilded Harvesters**. Even among other Pixies, this group is considered **EXTREMELY dangerous**. They live among normal Fey but secretly work to collect the souls and memories of mortals to fuel their own quest for forbidden knowledge.

### The Pixie Trade
Pixies do not value gold, gems, or land. Instead, they demand a price of **Extreme Value**—intangible aspects of a being's existence:
*   **The Weight of a Name:** Giving up one's true name, leading to a loss of identity.
*   **Capacity for Emotion:** The permanent loss of the ability to feel love, regret, or joy.
*   **Future Time:** A decade of life, or the memories of a firstborn.

### Motivation: The Harvest
The reason these artifacts are distributed is to facilitate **The Harvest**. 
1.  **Siphoning:** Artifacts are designed to siphon off a byproduct—fear, souls, or memories—which is funneled back to the Harvesters in the Feywild.
2.  **The Cost of Knowledge:** The Harvesters consume these memories to gain extreme knowledge, though this often leads to a specific type of madness (the "Bitter-Grin") as they become overwhelmed by the thousands of mortal lives they have consumed.
3.  **Memory Wiping:** They are known to aggressively use their memory-wiping abilities to protect the secret of the Harvest, making them a threat to any who investigate them.
