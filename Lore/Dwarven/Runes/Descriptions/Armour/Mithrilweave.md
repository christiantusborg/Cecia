# Mithrilweave

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Mithril** (The legendary silver-steel)
- **Vef** (Dwarven for 'Weave/Fabric')
> Meaning: "The Mithril Thread"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
The rune magically refines the armor's structure, making it lighter, more flexible, and less restrictive to the wearer's movements.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+15**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Rare**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** Reduces the armor's weight by **25%** and the **Armor Check Penalty by 1**.
- **Location 2:** Increases the **Maximum Dexterity Bonus** of the armor by **+1**.
- **Location 3:** Reduces the armor's weight by **50%** and the **ACP by 2**.
- **Location 4:** Increases the **Maximum Dexterity Bonus** by **+2**.
- **Location 5:** The armor's Arcane Spell Failure is reduced by **10%**.
- **Location 6:** The armor is treated as one category lighter (e.g., Medium becomes Light) for the purpose of movement and other limitations.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The armor has **no Maximum Dexterity Bonus** and **no Armor Check Penalty**.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is essential for agile warriors and casters who choose to wear armor.

---

## **Visual**
A delicate, silvery rune that looks like a fine lace pattern etched into the metal.

---

## **The Legend of the Mithrilweave**
- Forged by **Elara the Graceful**, a dwarf who studied elven weaving techniques to improve her clan's heavy plate.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Elara the Graceful:** A smith who believed that armor should be a second skin, not a prison.
