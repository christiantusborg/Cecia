# Rune of the Mimic

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Hermir** (Dwarven for 'Mimic/Imitator')
- **Búningr** (Dwarven for 'Suit/Outfit')
> Meaning: "The Rune of the Imitated Form"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
The armor can change its appearance at the wearer’s will, allowing it to look like common clothing, a different set of armor, or even a different person's gear.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wearer can change the armor's appearance to look like any other type of armor or clothing. It does not change the armor's properties.
- **Location 2:** The armor can change its texture, feeling like silk, leather, or iron to the touch.
- **Location 3:** The wearer gains a **+5 bonus** on Disguise checks while using the rune.
- **Location 4:** The armor can mimic the heraldry or insignia of a specific organization or individual perfectly.
- **Location 5:** The wearer can change their own physical appearance slightly (height, hair color) while wearing the armor (as per *Disguise Self*).
- **Location 6:** The armor can mimic the appearance of inanimate objects (like a statue or a chest) if the wearer remains still.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The armor can mimic the special properties of one other piece of magical armor it has touched for 24 hours.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This is an illusion effect (glamer).

---

## **Visual**
A kaleidoscopic rune that shifts colors rapidly when not "fixed" to a specific form.

---

## **The Legend of the Mimic**
- Created by **Loki the Trickster-Smith**, who loved to attend noble balls dressed as a common servant.
