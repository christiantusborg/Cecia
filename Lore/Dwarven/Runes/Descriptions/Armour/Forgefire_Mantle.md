# Forgefire Mantle

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Afl** (Dwarven for 'Forge')
- **Logi** (Dwarven for 'Fire/Flame')
> Meaning: "The Forge-Fire Cloak"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
The armor radiates the heat of a dwarf-forge, providing significant resistance to fire and even damaging those who strike the wearer.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wearer gains **Fire Resistance 5**.
- **Location 2:** Fire Resistance increases to **10**.
- **Location 3:** Any creature that strikes the wearer with a melee attack takes **1d4 fire damage**.
- **Location 4:** Fire Resistance increases to **15**.
- **Location 5:** Fire damage to attackers increases to **1d6**.
- **Location 6:** Fire Resistance increases to **20**. The wearer can cast *Produce Flame* at will.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The wearer is completely immune to non-magical fire and has **Fire Resistance 30**.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is highly favored by smiths and those who dwell near volcanoes.

---

## **Visual**
A series of flickering orange and red runes that seem to dance like flames across the armor's surface.

---

## **The Legend of the Forgefire Mantle**
- Created by **Gloin the Fire-Watcher**, who sought a way to work within the hottest depths of the Grand Forge without discomfort.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Gloin the Fire-Watcher:** A smith who was said to be born from a spark and felt more at home in the fire than in the air.
