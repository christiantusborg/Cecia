# Rune of the Phoenix

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Eldr** (Dwarven for 'Fire')
- **Fugl** (Dwarven for 'Bird')
> Meaning: "The Fire-Bird Rune"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
When the wearer is reduced to 0 hit points, they are instead reduced to 1 hit point and emit a burst of fire.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+25**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Very Rare**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** Once per long rest, if the wearer would be reduced to 0 HP, they stay at **1 HP**.
- **Location 2:** When the effect triggers, the wearer heals **1d10 HP**.
- **Location 3:** When the effect triggers, all enemies within 10 feet take **2d6 fire damage**.
- **Location 4:** Healing increases to **3d10 HP**.
- **Location 5:** Once per week, the effect can trigger a second time in the same day.
- **Location 6:** When the effect triggers, the wearer also gains the benefit of a *Fire Shield* spell for 1 minute.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] If the wearer dies, they are resurrected 24 hours later at the spot where they died, provided the armor is still intact.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is considered a "Life-Saving" rune and is extremely expensive to create.

---

## **Visual**
A beautiful, complex rune shaped like a bird with its wings spread, glowing with a brilliant gold and red light.

---

## **The Legend of the Phoenix**
- Legend says it was forged using a single feather from a celestial phoenix, gifted to a dwarven king who saved the bird's life.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Aurelius the Golden:** A smith who specialized in "High Magic" and sought to create artifacts of eternal life.
