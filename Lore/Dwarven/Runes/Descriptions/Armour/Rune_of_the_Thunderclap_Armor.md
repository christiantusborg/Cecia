# Rune of the Thunderclap (Armor)

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Duna** (Dwarven for 'Thunder')
- **Slagr** (Dwarven for 'Strike/Blow')
> Meaning: "The Resonant Armor"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
When the wearer is hit, the armor emits a thunderous clap that can deafen or even stun nearby enemies.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+15**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Rare**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** When hit, there is a **10% chance** to emit a thunderous clap. Enemies within 5 feet must succeed on a **DC 14 Fortitude save** or be *Deafened* for 1d4 rounds.
- **Location 2:** Chance increases to **25%**.
- **Location 3:** Radius increases to **10 feet**. Save DC increases to **16**.
- **Location 4:** Chance increases to **50%**.
- **Location 5:** On a failure, the enemy is also *Staggered* for 1 round.
- **Location 6:** Chance increases to **75%**. Save DC increases to **20**.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] Once per day, the wearer can trigger a *Great Shout* originating from themselves as a free action.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This is a sonic effect.

---

## **Visual**
Broad, flat runes that vibrate almost imperceptibly, glowing with a deep blue light.

---

## **The Legend of the Thunderclap**
- Created by **Bordin the Boisterous**, whose laugh was said to be loud enough to shake the walls of the Grand Forge.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Bordin the Boisterous:** A smith who loved the sound of his own hammer and sought to weaponize that resonance.
