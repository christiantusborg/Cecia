# Rune of the Sylph

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Vindr** (Dwarven for 'Wind')
- **Ganga** (Dwarven for 'Walk/Step')
> Meaning: "The Wind-Step Rune"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
The armor becomes as light as air, allowing the wearer to move silently and gracefully, even in heavy plate.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wearer gains a **+2 bonus** on Move Silently checks.
- **Location 2:** The armor check penalty for Move Silently is reduced by **2**.
- **Location 3:** The wearer gains a **+4 bonus** on Move Silently checks.
- **Location 4:** The wearer can cast *Silence* (centered on self) once per day for 1 minute.
- **Location 5:** The wearer gains a **+1 bonus** to AC against ranged attacks as the air around them deflects missiles.
- **Location 6:** The wearer can cast *Fly* once per day.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The wearer's footsteps make no sound at all, and they are immune to being tracked by scent.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is highly prized by spies and elven-dwarf hybrids.

---

## **Visual**
Wispy, swirling patterns that seem to flow across the armor like smoke.

---

## **The Legend of the Sylph**
- Legend says it was first created by a smith who fell in love with a wind-spirit and wanted to dance with her.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Zephyr the Light:** A smith who was famously thin and moved with an uncanny, airy grace.
