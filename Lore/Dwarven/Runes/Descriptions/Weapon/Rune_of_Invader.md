# Rune of the Invader

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Vaskr** (Dwarven for 'Valiant/Conquering')
- **Leið** (Dwarven for 'Path/Way')
> Meaning: "The Conqueror's Path"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
Improves navigation and tactical strategies in unfamiliar territories, drawing from the legendary expertise of conquering legions.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+5**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Common**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wielder gains a **+2 bonus** on Knowledge (Geography) and Survival checks to navigate.
- **Location 2:** The wielder gains a **+1 bonus** on Initiative checks when in a territory they do not "own."
- **Location 3:** The wielder can cast *Know Direction* at will.
- **Location 4:** The wielder gains a **+2 tactical bonus** to AC when they are flanking an enemy.
- **Location 5:** Once per day, the wielder can grant their allies within 30 feet a **+2 bonus** on attack rolls for 1 minute (as per the *Aid* spell).
- **Location 6:** The wielder is never considered "lost" and can always find their way back to a previously visited location.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] The weapon can project a map of the surrounding 5 miles into the wielder's mind.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is popular among explorers, scouts, and expansionist military leaders.

---

## **Visual**
A stylized boot stepping over a mountain, etched in copper or bronze.

---

## **The Legend of the Invader**
- First used by the **Legions of the East** as they pushed into the wild frontiers of Cecia.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **General Hektor:** A retired military commander who turned to smithing to ensure his successors had the tools he lacked.
