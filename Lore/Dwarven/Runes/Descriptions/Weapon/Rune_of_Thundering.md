# Rune of Thundering

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Thrum** (Dwarven for 'Noise/Uproar')
- **Galdr** (Dwarven for 'Spell/Sound')
> Meaning: "The Spell of the Roaring Sound"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
The weapon emits a deafening boom upon a successful critical hit, potentially stunning the target and shattering their resolve.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** On a critical hit, the target must succeed on a **DC 14 Fortitude save** or be **deafened** for 1d4 rounds.
- **Location 2:** Save DC increases to **16**.
- **Location 3:** Save DC increases to **18**.
- **Location 4:** On a critical hit, the target must succeed on a **DC 20 Fortitude save** or be **stunned** for 1 round and deafened for 1d6 rounds.
- **Location 5:** Save DC increases to **22**.
- **Location 6:** Save DC increases to **24**. Stun duration increases to **1d3 rounds**.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] The sound is so intense it deals an additional **3d6 sonic damage** on a critical hit, regardless of the save.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- Deafened creatures take a -4 penalty on initiative and have a 20% chance of spell failure when casting spells with verbal components.

#### **4.4.1 Roleplay Rules (Optional)**
- The weapon vibrates slightly when enemies are nearby, emitting a low hum.

#### **4.4.2 Critical Hit Effects (Optional)**
- **Echoing Boom:** The sound can be heard up to a mile away in quiet conditions.

---

## **Visual**
The rune consists of jagged, lightning-like lines radiating from a central point. When it triggers, a visible ripple in the air expands from the point of impact.

---

## **The Legend of the Thundering Rune**
- Created by **Haldor Deepshout**, a dwarf who lost his hearing in a forge accident and sought to make the world "feel" the sound he could no longer hear.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Haldor Deepshout:** A jovial but incredibly loud smith who communicated primarily through rhythmic hammering.
