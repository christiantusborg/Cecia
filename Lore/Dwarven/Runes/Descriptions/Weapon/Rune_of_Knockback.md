# Rune of Knockback

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Hrinda** (Dwarven for 'Push/Knock')
- **Aptr** (Dwarven for 'Back')
> Meaning: "The Rune of the Backward Push"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
Whenever a knockback weapon hits its target, it initiates a bull rush attack in addition to dealing normal damage.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** On a hit, the weapon initiates a **Bull Rush** attack. Treat the projectile/weapon as a Medium creature with a **+4 Strength bonus**.
- **Location 2:** Strength bonus increases to **+6**.
- **Location 3:** The weapon is treated as a **Large creature** for the Bull Rush.
- **Location 4:** Strength bonus increases to **+8**.
- **Location 5:** On a critical hit, the target is also knocked **prone** if the bull rush is successful.
- **Location 6:** The weapon is treated as a **Huge creature** for the Bull Rush with a **+10 Strength bonus**.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] The knockback is so powerful it deals **1d6 extra damage** for every 10 feet the target is pushed.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- The wielder does not move with the target.
- For ranged weapons, this ability is bestowed upon the ammunition.

---

## **Visual**
A horizontal arrow pointing backwards, with several vertical lines representing force.

---

## **The Legend of the Knockback**
- First used by the **Defenders of the Gates** to keep invaders from reaching the top of the ramparts.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Gimli the Stout:** A smith who was tired of enemies getting "too close for comfort."
