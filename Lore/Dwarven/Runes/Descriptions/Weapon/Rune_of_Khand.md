# Rune of Khand

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Khand** (Foreign name of a distant, curved-blade culture)
- **Skar** (Dwarven for 'Cut')
> Meaning: "The Curved Cut of Khand"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
Designed for curved blades and swift, sweeping strikes, this rune enhances the fluidity and reach of the weapon.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The weapon's reach is increased by **5 feet** during the wielder's turn only.
- **Location 2:** The wielder gains a **+1 bonus** on attack rolls made after a move action.
- **Location 3:** The weapon deals an additional **1d6 slashing damage** on a charge.
- **Location 4:** Reach increases by 5 feet permanently.
- **Location 5:** The wielder gains the *Cleave* feat while the weapon is drawn.
- **Location 6:** On a successful hit, the target must succeed on a **DC 18 Reflex save** or be *Hamstrung* (-10 ft speed).
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] Every attack is a sweeping blow, striking all enemies within reach for half damage if the primary attack hits.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- Favored by riders and those who fight in open spaces.

---

## **Visual**
A sweeping, crescent-shaped rune that glows with a faint, desert-sunset orange light.

---

## **The Legend of the Khand**
- Brought to the dwarven kingdoms by **Riders of the Far East** who sought to combine their curved steel with dwarven runes.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Tamerlane the Curver:** A smith who studied the aerodynamics of blades to create the perfect "flying" strike.
