# Rune of Thunderclap

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Duna** (Dwarven for 'Thunder')
- **Slagr** (Dwarven for 'Strike/Blow')
> Meaning: "The Thunderous Strike"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
On a critical hit, the weapon creates a localized shockwave of sonic energy, damaging and pushing back nearby foes.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+15**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Rare**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** On a critical hit, all creatures within **5 feet** (except the wielder) take **1d6 sonic damage**.
- **Location 2:** Damage increases to **2d6**.
- **Location 3:** Radius increases to **10 feet**.
- **Location 4:** Damage increases to **3d6**. Affected creatures must succeed on a **DC 15 Fortitude save** or be pushed 5 feet back.
- **Location 5:** Damage increases to **4d6**. Save DC increases to **18**.
- **Location 6:** Damage increases to **5d6**. Radius increases to **15 feet**. Save DC increases to **20**.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] The shockwave also acts as a *Great Shout* spell in a 30-foot cone originating from the target.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This effect can only trigger once per round.

#### **4.4.1 Roleplay Rules (Optional)**
- Using this rune in a confined space (like a small tunnel) may cause minor ceiling collapses or falling debris.

---

## **Visual**
A broad, flat rune that resembles a stylized hammer. It glows with a deep blue light that pulses in sync with the wielder's heartbeat.

---

## **The Legend of the Thunderclap**
- Used by the defenders of **Karak-Azul** to break the charges of goblin hordes in the narrowest passageways.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Thrain Stormborn:** An eccentric wizard-smith who spent years living on a mountain peak to study the nature of storms.
