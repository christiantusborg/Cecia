# Rune of Soul Forcing

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Sál** (Dwarven for 'Soul')
- **Thryngva** (Dwarven for 'Force/Compel')
> Meaning: "The Soul-Forcer"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
A darker variant of the binding rune, this rune is used to forcibly trap a soul into a weapon crystal, often against its will, to exploit its power.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+30**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Legendary (Forbidden)**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** Once per day, on a killing blow, the rune attempts to trap the target's soul (Will DC 15). If successful, the weapon deals an extra **1d6 necrotic damage** for 24 hours.
- **Location 2:** Extra damage increases to **2d6**.
- **Location 3:** The trapped soul can be "consumed" to grant the wielder temporary hit points equal to the soul's CR.
- **Location 4:** Save DC increases to **20**. The extra damage is now always active if a soul is trapped.
- **Location 5:** The wielder can force the soul to use one of its own abilities (as a 4th-level spell effect).
- **Location 6:** Extra damage increases to **3d6**. The soul is slowly consumed, lasting only 1 month before a new one is needed.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] The weapon can "store" up to three souls at once, combining their powers into a devastating aura of dread.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This is considered an **Evil** act and a forbidden rune among most dwarven clans.
- The trapped soul may attempt to resist or curse the wielder.

---

## **Visual**
Jagged, aggressive purple runes that seem to "pinch" or "constrict" the crystal they are etched upon.

---

## **The Legend of the Soul Forcing**
- Used by the **Duergar of the Deep Dark** to create weapons of pure agony that could shatter the minds of their enemies.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Zarkon the Cruel:** A renegade smith who was exiled for his experiments on living captives.
