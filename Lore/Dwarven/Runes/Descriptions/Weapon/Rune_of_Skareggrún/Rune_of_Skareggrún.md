# Rune of Skareggrún

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Skar** (Dwarven for 'Sharp/Cutting')
- **Eggr** (Dwarven for 'Edge')
- **Rún** (Dwarven for 'Rune/Mark')
> Meaning: "The Rune of the Honed Edge"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
This rune focuses the magical essence of the forge into the very molecule of the blade's edge, drastically increasing the likelihood of landing a devastating blow.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Rare**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The weapon's threat range for critical hits is **doubled**. (For example, a threat range of 19–20 becomes 17–20).
- **Location 2:** **N/A** (No effect)
- **Location 3:** **N/A** (No effect)
- **Location 4:** **N/A** (No effect)
- **Location 5:** **N/A** (No effect)
- **Location 6:** The weapon's threat range for critical hits is **tripled**. This effect represents a perfect synchronization of multiple refinement methods, allowing the blade to find vital points with supernatural ease. (For example, a threat range of 19–20 becomes 15–20).
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] **N/A** (No effect)

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This effect does not stack with other magical or feat-based refinements to the threat range; it is a singular, absolute state of the blade's edge.

#### **4.4.1 Roleplay Rules (Optional)**
- **Fragile Perfection:** The edge is so fine that it can be damaged if used to strike inanimate objects like stone or heavy iron doors (requires a DC 15 Craft: Weaponsmithing check to maintain).

#### **4.4.2 Critical Hit Effects (Optional)**
- **Clean Cut:** On a critical hit, the weapon ignores 5 points of any physical Damage Reduction the target may possess.

---

## **Visual**
The rune is a single, perfectly straight horizontal line that glows with a blinding white light. On the sword, the edge itself seems to disappear when viewed head-on, becoming so thin it almost vanishes from sight.

---

## **The Legend of the Skareggrún**
- First etched by **Balin Ironedge**, who sought to create a blade that could cut a silk scarf dropped across its edge. He spent forty years refining the geometry of the rune until it achieved a state of "infinite thinness" at the sixth slot of his personal greatsword.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Balin Ironedge:** A perfectionist whose workshop was filled with magnifying crystals and ultra-fine whetstones. He was famously impatient with "clumsy" warriors, believing that a sword should be a needle of death, not a club of iron. He was said to have eventually cut a hole in reality itself with his final masterpiece.
