# Rune of Phoenix's Flame

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Fönix** (Dwarven adaptation for 'Phoenix')
- **Eld** (Dwarven for 'Fire/Flame')
- **Rún** (Dwarven for 'Rune')
> Meaning: "The Fire-Breath of Rebirth"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
This powerful defensive rune acts as a spiritual anchor. If the wielder is slain while holding the weapon, the rune consumes itself to return the wielder to life, causing a structural shift in the weapon's remaining runic matrix.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+20** (Hard)

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Legendary**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1-6:** If the wielder is reduced to -10 hit points or otherwise killed while holding the weapon, they are immediately restored to life as per the *Raise Dead* spell. This effect triggers automatically. 
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only] **Cannot be placed here.** The complex soul-binding of a Slot 7 weapon is too volatile to support a Phoenix-Flame anchor.

---

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules: The Runic Collapse**
When the Rune of Phoenix's Flame is consumed, it leaves a "void" in the weapon's sequence. To maintain the weapon's integrity, all runes in slots *higher* than the consumed Phoenix Rune immediately shift down one slot.
- **The Shift:** If the Phoenix Rune was in Slot 4, the rune in Slot 5 moves to Slot 4, Slot 6 moves to Slot 5, and Slot 7 moves to Slot 6.
- **Incompatible Shifts:** If a shifted rune moves into a slot where it is mechanically "N/A" or "Cannot be placed here" (according to its own rules), that rune is **destroyed** and its magic dissipates instantly.
- **One-Time Use:** Once the rebirth is triggered, the Phoenix Rune is erased from the blade entirely.

#### **4.4.1 Roleplay Rules (Optional)**
- **The Debt of Fire:** Those brought back by this rune often have hair that turns charcoal black or eyes that flicker like embers for several weeks.

#### **4.4.2 Critical Hit Effects (Optional)**
- **Warmth of Life:** While the rune is active (unused), the wielder gains a +2 bonus on all Fortitude saves against death effects.

---

## **Visual**
The rune is a complex, swirling pattern of oranges and reds that looks like a bird mid-flight. It feels warm to the touch, even when the blade is sheathed. When it triggers, the blade erupts in a pillar of blinding white fire that envelops the wielder.

---

## **The Legend of the Phoenix's Flame**
- First forged by **Thrain the Devoted**, who couldn't bear to see his King fall in the Battle of the Broken Peaks. He sacrificed his own masterwork hammer to provide the "spark" for the first Phoenix Rune. The King lived, but Thrain's forge never burned as bright again.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Thrain the Devoted:** A smith known more for his loyalty than his ambition. He believed that a weapon's greatest purpose wasn't to take life, but to preserve the ones worth savin'. He died a pauper, havin' given away his most powerful creations to the guardians of the deep halls.
