# Rune of the Rose

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Rós** (Dwarven for 'Rose')
- **Gróa** (Dwarven for 'Heal/Grow')
> Meaning: "The Healing Bloom"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
Heals or rejuvenates the wearer over time, providing a constant source of life energy.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wearer gains **Fast Healing 1** while the armor is worn, up to half their maximum HP.
- **Location 2:** The wearer is immune to **Bleeding** effects.
- **Location 3:** Fast Healing increases to **2**, and works up to full HP.
- **Location 4:** Once per day, the wearer can cast *Cure Light Wounds*.
- **Location 5:** The wearer gains a **+4 bonus** on saves against poison and disease.
- **Location 6:** Once per day, the wearer can cast *Restoration*.
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The wearer gains **Fast Healing 5** and can never die from old age.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This rune is often found on the armor of knights and healers.

---

## **Visual**
A beautiful, blooming rose with thorns, glowing with a soft pinkish-red light.

---

## **The Legend of the Rose**
- Legend says it was first grown by a druid-smith who planted a magical rose in the center of his forge.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Flora the Gentle:** A female smith who believed that metal could be as nurturing as the earth.
