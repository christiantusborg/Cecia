# Rune of Thurisaz

**Combining (Name Etymology):**
- **Thurisaz** (The rune for 'Giant/Thorn/Protection')
> Meaning: "The Protection of the Thorn"

---

## **Short Description or Core Effect**
Serves as a protective ward, granting the wearer a bonus to their natural armor.

---

## **Game Rules**

### **4.1 Rune Difficulty**
**+10**

### **4.2 Rune Rarity**
**Uncommon**

### **4.3 Effect Variation Based on Location**
- **Location 1:** The wearer gains a **+2 natural armor bonus to AC**.
- **Location 2:** **N/A**
- **Location 3:** Any creature that strikes the wearer with an unarmed attack or a natural weapon takes **1d4 piercing damage**.
- **Location 4:** **N/A**
- **Location 5:** The natural armor bonus increases to **+4**.
- **Location 6:** **N/A**
- **Location 7:** [Soul Swords Only - applied to Armor] The natural armor bonus increases to **+6**.

### **4.4 Additional or Specific Rules**
- This bonus stacks with the wearer's existing natural armor, but not with other magical natural armor bonuses.

---

## **Visual**
The Thurisaz rune (resembling a thorn), glowing with a sharp white-grey light.

---

## **The Legend of Thurisaz**
- Inspired by the iron-thorns that grow in the **Gardens of Eisenwacht**, which are said to be indestructible.

---

## **Description of Creator**
- **Thorn-Smith Bjorn:** A smith who believed that the best defense was one that also hurt the attacker.
